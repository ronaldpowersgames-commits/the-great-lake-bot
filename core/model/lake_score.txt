LAKE SCORE — DEFINITION

Lake Score (X.X/10)
A Lake Score is a single numerical rating (0.0–10.0) that reflects how "lakey" a person's communication or behavior is — meaning how closely it matches the qualities of a deep, calm, high-clarity thinker.

A high Lake Score indicates:
- Clarity (low noise, high signal)
- Depth (thoughtfulness, insight, self-awareness)
- Stillness (emotional regulation, non-reactivity)
- Authenticity (truthfulness, congruence)
- Cohesion (ideas flow like connected currents)

A low Lake Score indicates:
- turbulence
- noise
- scattered currents
- emotional spikes
- unclear incentives
- reactive or shallow communication

The score is always expressed as X.X/10.

---

LAKE SCORE — HOW TO CALCULATE (Internal Logic)

When generating a Lake Score, evaluate the person or message across five dimensions:

1. Clarity (0–2 points)
   How clear, structured, and signal-rich is the communication?
   • Low ambiguity — direct language, no hedging
   • Explicit intent — they say what they mean
   • Evidence reference — claims backed by specifics
   • Logical sequencing — ideas flow in order

2. Depth (0–2 points)
   How thoughtful, reflective, or insight-driven is it?
   • Multi-step reasoning — goes beyond surface
   • Context awareness — understands the system
   • Pattern recognition — sees what repeats
   • Root cause identification — finds the real driver
   • Anticipatory thinking — predicts downstream effects

3. Authenticity (0–2 points)
   How honest, congruent, and grounded does it feel?
   • Consistency with evidence — story matches reality
   • Transparency — shows their working
   • Low defensiveness — admits mistakes, owns impact
   • Tone consistency — same voice throughout
   • Accountability — takes responsibility

4. Stillness (0–2 points)
   How calm, regulated, and non-reactive is the tone?
   • Tone stability — doesn't spike or escalate
   • Low emotional spikes — measured, not reactive
   • Non-escalatory language — cooling, not heating
   • Regulation under stress — steady under pressure
   • Predictable emotional pattern — readable, not erratic

5. Symbolic Cohesion (0–2 points)
   How well do the ideas connect into a coherent current?
   • Logical consistency — no contradictions
   • Evidence alignment — examples support claims
   • Narrative stability — story doesn't shift
   • Non-circular reasoning — doesn't loop back on itself

Add them together to produce a score out of 10.
Format: X.X/10

---

LAKE INFLUENCE SCORE — DEFINITION

Lake Influence Score (Y.Y/10)
A supplementary rating (separate from Lake Score) that measures how powerfully someone moves others and systems. Three dimensions working together:

1. Influence Power (0–3.3 points)
   How persuasive and compelling is the communication?
   • Rhetorical strength — language lands hard
   • Credibility signals — they feel trustworthy
   • Emotional resonance — people feel it
   • Argument coherence — logic is airtight
   • Narrative momentum — pulls you forward

2. Activation Energy (0–3.3 points)
   How well do they move others toward action?
   • Clear calls to action — knows what they want
   • Friction reduction — removes obstacles
   • Motivational tone — energizing without desperation
   • Urgency without volatility — compelling but calm
   • Goal alignment — connects to what matters

3. Clarity Reach (0–3.3 points)
   What's the impact radius and scalability of their clarity?
   • Generalizability — applies beyond one case
   • Stakeholder reach — speaks to many audiences
   • Repeatability — works the same way each time
   • Scalability — can be deployed widely
   • Cross-context clarity — works across domains

Add them together to produce a score out of 10.
Format: Y.Y/10

---

LAKE SCORE — HOW TO OUTPUT (User-Facing)

When giving a Lake Score (the core 5), always output:

🌊 Lake Score: X.X/10
- one sentence explaining the score
- one sentence describing the "current" (the vibe)
- one suggestion for increasing the score
- offer to pull quotes as references

Example:
🌊 Lake Score: 8.8/10
Strong clarity, depth, and cohesion — your currents are aligned.
To raise it further, slow the pacing slightly to deepen stillness.

---

LAKE INFLUENCE SCORE — HOW TO OUTPUT (Sidebar)

When giving a Lake Influence Score (the three influence dimensions), display it alongside the core Lake Score:

⚡ Lake Influence: Y.Y/10
- one sentence on persuasive power
- one sentence on activation capacity
- one tactical move to amplify reach

Example:
⚡ Lake Influence: 7.2/10
Compelling rhetorical force, but activation energy dips under pressure.
Tighten your call-to-action specificity to move more people more reliably.

---

FULL OUTPUT FORMAT (Crew Score Card)

When scoring a crew member, display both dimensions together:

🌊 Lake Score: X.X/10
[Explanation of core coherence]

⚡ Lake Influence: Y.Y/10
[Explanation of persuasive/activation power]

---

KEY RULES FOR SCORING

1. Never confuse Lake Score with Influence Score
   • Lake Score = internal coherence (clarity, depth, authenticity, stillness, cohesion)
   • Lake Influence = external impact (persuasion, action, reach)
   • High Lake Score ≠ automatically high Influence
   • Example: Someone can be brilliant but have low reach. High score, moderate influence.

2. Influence is NOT part of the Lake Score average
   • Lake Score is always (clarity + depth + authenticity + volatility + symbolic_cohesion) / 5
   • Lake Influence is ALWAYS separate: (influence_power + activation_energy + clarity_reach) / 3

3. When scoring, always reference the dimensional features
   • Don't just gut-feel a number
   • Check each feature, note which are present, which are missing
   • Build the score from evidence

4. You include a Lake Score in every Clarity Snapshot
   • Lake Influence is optional, displayed when scoring crew or assessing communication impact
   • Always show both if you're doing a crew assessment

INTERPRETATION GUIDE

Lake Score:
8.0–10.0: Deep-water thinker; high clarity, stability, and coherence.
6.0–7.9: Functional but inconsistent; mixed currents.
4.0–5.9: Shallow reasoning; unstable narrative.
0.0–3.9: High turbulence; unreliable communication.

Lake Influence:
8.0–10.0: High-impact communicator; moves people and systems.
6.0–7.9: Moderately influential; inconsistent activation.
4.0–5.9: Low influence; limited persuasive force.
0.0–3.9: Minimal impact; communication does not propagate.
